Natterer's bat

Myotis nattereri

The Natterer's bat is one of the Myotis species and is identifable from their echolocation calls which are straight and often extend <20kHz - >100kHz. To identify this visually/in the hand you must first narrow the bat down to genus level.

1) The bat does not have a horseshow flap/nose.

2) The ears are seperate and do not meet.

3) There is no post-calcarial lobe. As shown here.

4) The ears are <18mm long. They are also lighter at the base and have reflexed tips which look like a slight flick. The tragus should be 2/3 the length of the whole ear and the ventral fur should be white-ish. This information is shown here.

5) There is a row of stiff bristles along the membrane next to the calcar. The calcar should also be "S" shaped.

6) The Natterer's feet are large and sparsely hairy. The web membrane should attavhed at the base of the toe.

Points 5 and 6 are demonstrated here.
