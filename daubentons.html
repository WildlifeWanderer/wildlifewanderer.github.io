Daubenton's bat

Myotis daubentonii

The Daubenton's (also known as the water bat) is identifiable through it's calls with combing, and a strong heel at 45khz, the following features can be used to identify it in the hand:

1) The bat does not have a horseshow flap/nose.

2) The ears are seperate and do not meet.

3) There is no post-calcarial lobe. As shown here.

4) The ears are rounded and leathery. As shown here

5) The ears are <18mm and are lighter at the base. The tragus is <1/2 the length of the ear and is blunt at the tip.

6) There is a pink face and bare patch around the eye and it's ventral fur is white/grey, even in length and wooly.

Points 5 and 6 are demonstrated here.

7) It has large hairy feet and the membrane attaches in the middle of the foot. As shown here.

8) There are no stiff bristles (as with Natterer's) and the calcar is long and straigh extending for ~3/4 of the tail membrane, as can be seen here.
